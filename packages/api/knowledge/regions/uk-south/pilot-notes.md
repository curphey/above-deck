# UK South Coast — Pilot Notes

Covering the English Channel coast from Land's End to the North Foreland.

## The Solent

Strong tidal streams throughout. The key tidal gate is **Hurst Narrows**: maximum spring rate 4.5 knots, ebbing west-going. Plan to arrive at the narrows at or near slack water unless you have adequate power. The **Needles Channel** is the western approach: adequate water in the main channel but avoid the shallows to the north of the Bridge ledge. The North Channel around the back of the Isle of Wight is quieter but shallower; leave Ryde Middle buoy well to port inbound.

## Portland Bill

The race off Portland Bill is one of the most dangerous on the UK coast. In wind-against-tide conditions the sea state can be extreme with steep, breaking waves. Two safe options: pass **inshore** through the inner passage (viable in calm conditions, good visibility, and neap tides only) or give the race at least **3 nautical miles** of clearance offshore. Avoid the race at springs in any wind against tide.

## English Channel Shipping Lanes

The Dover Strait TSS carries the highest density of commercial traffic in the world. The east-bound lane runs to the northeast; the west-bound lane runs to the southwest. Cross the TSS at a right angle (090°/270° heading, not course-made-good) as required by Rule 10 COLREGs. Monitor Channel 16 and maintain a continuous watch. Yachts are strongly advised to cross at night or in good visibility only.

## Firing Ranges

**Lulworth Range** — the range along the Purbeck coast is active most weekdays and some weekends. Red flags and range safety vessels indicate firing is in progress; pass seaward of safety vessels. Check NAVTEX (B and K stations) and the range office VHF for clearance times. Do not enter the range area when red flags are displayed.

## Key Approaches

- **Falmouth** — straightforward deep-water approach, well marked; enter via Black Rock buoy
- **Plymouth Sound** — use Drake Channel for the main approach; the Western Channel is shoal at the edges; Breakwater Fort light on approach
- **Dartmouth** — narrow entrance with a sill; entrance rock Checkstone is close to the starboard hand; proceed slowly, favour the centre

## Anchorages

- **Studland Bay** — good holding in sand, sheltered from westerlies; exposed to north and east
- **Lulworth Cove** — well sheltered but entry is tight; firing range adjacent — check clearance
- **Chapman's Pool** — attractive secluded anchorage; open to south and southeast; deteriorates quickly in southerly swell

## Tidal Considerations

Portland Harbour uses **Portland tide tables**. The Solent has a double high water; use dedicated Solent tide tables. For the central and eastern Channel, apply the **Dover tide tables** with appropriate corrections. Springs run significantly stronger than neaps throughout; plan tidal gates accordingly.

## Weather

BBC Shipping Forecast sea areas covering UK South: **Portland** (western English Channel), **Wight** (central and eastern English Channel), **Dover** (Strait of Dover). For inshore passages, use the **BBC Inshore Waters Forecast** broadcast on Channel 67. The Met Office issues Inshore Forecast at 0033, 0633, 1233, and 1833 UTC on Channel 67. Summer sea breezes can significantly increase afternoon wind strength along the coastal strip.
