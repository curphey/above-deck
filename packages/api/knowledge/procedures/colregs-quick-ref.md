# COLREGS Quick Reference

International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea (COLREGs 1972).

## Rule 5 — Lookout

Every vessel shall at all times maintain a proper look-out by sight and hearing, and by all other available means, so as to make a full appraisal of the situation and the risk of collision.

## Rule 7 — Risk of Collision

Use all available means appropriate to prevailing circumstances, including radar, AIS, and compass bearings. A risk of collision shall be deemed to exist if the compass bearing to an approaching vessel does not appreciably change.

## Rule 8 — Action to Avoid Collision

Action must be positive, made in ample time, and large enough to be readily apparent to the other vessel. Avoid a succession of small alterations. Ensure the action results in passing at a safe distance; check that it is effective until the other vessel is finally past and clear.

## Rule 12 — Sailing Vessels

When two sailing vessels are approaching one another: the vessel on the port tack gives way to the vessel on the starboard tack. When both on the same tack, the windward vessel gives way to the leeward vessel.

## Rule 13 — Overtaking

An overtaking vessel must keep clear. A vessel is overtaking if she is approaching from a direction more than 22.5° abaft the beam of the vessel ahead.

## Rule 14 — Head-On Situation

When two power-driven vessels are meeting head-on or nearly head-on, both shall alter course to starboard so each passes on the port side of the other.

## Rule 15 — Crossing Situation

The vessel which has the other on her starboard side is the give-way vessel. The stand-on vessel maintains course and speed.

## Rule 18 — Responsibilities Between Vessels

Priority order (highest to lowest right-of-way): Not Under Command (NUC) > Restricted in Ability to Manoeuvre (RAM) > Constrained by Draught (CBD) > Sailing vessel > Power-driven vessel underway > Fishing vessel (not at anchor). All vessels must avoid NUC and RAM vessels.

## Rule 19 — Restricted Visibility

There is no stand-on vessel in restricted visibility. Every vessel must take action to avoid collision in ample time. Safe speed must be reduced. On detecting another vessel by radar only, avoid alteration to port for a vessel forward of the beam.
