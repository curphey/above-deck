## The Problem

Every cruising sailor faces the same challenge when setting up or upgrading their electrical system: how much solar do I actually need? How big should my battery bank be? Will my watermaker work with my current setup?

The answers exist — scattered across forum threads from 2014, YouTube videos of varying accuracy, and expensive marine electricians who may or may not understand your specific boat. There's no single place where a sailor can model their actual setup with real equipment specs and get reliable numbers.

## What We're Building

Above Deck is an open-source platform for cruising sailors. We started with the problem we know best: energy planning.

The **Energy Planner** lets you model your boat's electrical system from scratch. Select your actual equipment — real Schenker watermakers, actual Victron panels, genuine Raymarine electronics — and see exactly how much power you need, how much solar to install, and whether your battery bank is large enough.

No spreadsheets. No guessing. No "it depends" without the numbers to back it up.

## Why Open Source

Marine software tends to be either free-but-terrible or expensive-and-locked-down. We think there's a better model: build useful tools in the open, let the community contribute corrections and improvements, and sustain the project through the value it creates rather than paywalls.

Every equipment spec in our database is sourced from manufacturer datasheets with provenance URLs. If a number is wrong, anyone can check our sources and submit a correction. That's how you build trust in a community of people who depend on accurate information.

## What's Next

The Energy Planner is our starting point. Here's what we're working on:

- **Knowledge Base** — Practical guides for every system on your boat, written by experienced cruisers and technically verified.
- **Community** — Discussion threads organised by topic, not lost in the noise of general sailing forums.
- **Equipment Reviews** — Real-world data from sailors using the gear, not sponsored content.

## Get Involved

This project only works if sailors contribute. Try the [Energy Planner](/tools/solar), tell us what's wrong, suggest equipment we're missing, and share your real-world consumption data.

Every correction makes the tool better for the next sailor planning their system. That's the point.
